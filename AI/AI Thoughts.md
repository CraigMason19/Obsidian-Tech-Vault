## Strengths, Weaknesses & Societal Impact
### Strengths

- Good for sound boarding, rubber ducking
- Never judges, no question is silly
- Fantastic at overviews
- Fantastic at naming things like classes and variables
- Fantastic at debugging
- Fantastic at fixing software problems (not coding, just using)
- Can be used a teacher (to set questions or a problem) to increase understanding
- I use it more than google
- Wide range (code, Greek Myth etc...)
- Can extract text from scans or images
- Allows for thinking freely, without redefining basic concepts.
- You can see the 'trending word or topic'
### Weaknesses 

- Frame‑Locking (the model can’t reset itself mid‑conversation) Frame‑Locking is good, our thread tonight, I can recall but midgear shifts in thinking you struggle with.
- Over eager
- Often too complimentary
- Often wrong, unless you explicitly state context first
- A thread can be 'poisoned' by what it thinks is correct, if wrong it will still focus on that on use that as reference, So often starting anew conversation without previous context is better
	- Once a false frame is set, it becomes the load-bearing assumption
- Doesn’t challenge you (any insane thought i had and you'd have to be nice)
- Only responds to what I give
- Sycophantic. Really annoying
- Once it learns something, it wont let go of that framing
- You have to know the topic to get meaningful results
- Addictive
### Societal Impact

- Ai is here. We have to live together.
- Resource Intensive
- Pushes up GPU, RAM prices etc...
- Over reliance, not double checked or scrutinised
- Treated as gospel. Many accept it as truth
- garbage input / output. Only as good as information and context given
- anthropomorphize (automamorphizing, like talos, the automaton)
- A lack of criticism gives false confidence
- Just producing content, quality be damned
- People think they can create, where really they produce a amalgamation other creators made for you. Midjourney did that from others art
